PDF Line Fixer

Paste text copied from a PDF — choose a mode below to fix broken line breaks.

Smart Merge — The tool automatically analyses text structure to decide where the real paragraph breaks are. It looks at punctuation, capitalisation, numbering patterns (e.g. (a), 1., Section), and line length to distinguish real paragraph breaks from false ones introduced by the PDF column width. Best for multi-paragraph text.
Manual Markers — You decide where the paragraph breaks are. Type == anywhere in the text to mark a paragraph break. All line breaks are removed; only your == markers create paragraph breaks.
Single Paragraph — Use this when you copied exactly one paragraph from a PDF. All line breaks are removed and the text is joined into one continuous paragraph. No analysis needed.
Type == anywhere you want a paragraph break — it can be inline, at the end of a line, or on its own line. Everything between markers is merged into one paragraph. Example:

This is the first paragraph which wraps across two lines.==This is the second paragraph which also wraps.

Or across lines:

First paragraph line one
line two of first paragraph.==
Second paragraph line one
line two of second paragraph.
